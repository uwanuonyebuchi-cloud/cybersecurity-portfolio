#  Cybersecurity Risk Matrix

## Overview

This lab demonstrates how to assess and prioritize cybersecurity risks using a **5×5 Risk Matrix**. A risk matrix is a widely used Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) tool that helps organizations evaluate risks based on their **Likelihood** and **Impact**, allowing security teams to prioritize remediation efforts.

---

## Objective

The objective of this lab was to:

- Understand cybersecurity risk assessment principles.
- Evaluate risks using likelihood and impact ratings.
- Classify risks into Low, Medium, High, and Critical categories.
- Learn how organizations prioritize security risks based on business impact.

## Risk Matrix Structure

The matrix uses two factors to determine the overall risk level:

### Likelihood

- Rare (1)
- Unlikely (2)
- Possible (3)
- Likely (4)
- Almost Certain (5)

### Impact

- Negligible (1)
- Minor (2)
- Moderate (3)
- Major (4)
- Catastrophic (5)

The intersection of these values determines the overall risk rating.

---

## Risk Levels

### Low Risk
Minimal impact with a low probability of occurring.

### Medium Risk
Requires monitoring and appropriate mitigation.

### High Risk
Requires prompt remediation to reduce organizational risk.

### Critical Risk
Requires immediate action due to significant business impact.
