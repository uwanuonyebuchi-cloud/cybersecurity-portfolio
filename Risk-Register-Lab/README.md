# Cybersecurity Risk Register

## Overview

This lab demonstrates the process of identifying, assessing, and documenting cybersecurity risks using a Risk Register. A risk register is a key governance and risk management (GRC) document that helps organizations track security risks, determine their likelihood and impact, assign ownership, and define mitigation strategies.

---

## Objective

The objective of this lab was to practice identifying common cybersecurity risks, evaluating their severity using a risk matrix, and documenting appropriate mitigation plans.

---

## Lab Environment

- Microsoft Excel
- Windows 11
- GitHub

---

## Risk Assessment Process

The following steps were performed during this lab:

1. Identified potential cybersecurity risks.
2. Determined the likelihood of each risk occurring.
3. Evaluated the business impact if the risk occurred.
4. Assigned an overall risk rating (Low, Medium, High, or Critical).
5. Documented mitigation and remediation strategies.
6. Recorded the current status of each risk.

---

## Sample Risks Identified

Examples of risks included in the register:

- Phishing attacks
- Weak passwords
- Ransomware infection
- Malware
- Insider threats
- Unpatched operating systems
- Unauthorized access
- Data breaches
- Lost or stolen devices
- Misconfigured firewall rules

---

## Risk Mitigation Examples

Examples of recommended security controls include:

- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Security Awareness Training
- Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)
- Regular Patch Management
- Daily Data Backups
- Network Firewalls
- Email Filtering
- Least Privilege Access
- Vulnerability Scanning
- Incident Response Procedures
